What is AI? The three horizons

Artificial intelligence mimics human cognition — learning, reasoning, and self-correction. Its spectrum ranges from narrow tools to hypothetical superintelligence.

Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI)

Today’s reality: AI specialized in one task. Facial recognition, language translators, and recommendation engines. ANI powers your digital world.

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

Human‑level adaptability: a single system that can learn any intellectual task. Still on the horizon, AGI would reason, plan, and solve problems like a person.

Artificial Superintelligence (ASI)

Beyond human brilliance: ASI would surpass the best minds in science, creativity, and social skills. A concept that fuels both hope and ethical debate.

Milestones on the AI timeline

From theory to global phenomenon — the journey so far.
1950

Turing Test

Alan Turing proposes a test of machine intelligence: can a machine imitate human conversation?

1956

Dartmouth workshop

The birth of AI as a field; the term "artificial intelligence" is coined.

1997

Deep Blue defeats Kasparov

IBM’s chess computer beats world champion, showing machines can outcalculate humans.

2012

Deep learning breakthrough

AlexNet wins ImageNet; GPUs ignite the neural network revolution.

2022

ChatGPT & generative AI

LLMs bring AI to the masses — creating text, images, and code with human-like fluency.

Today

Agentic AI & beyond

Autonomous systems that plan and execute tasks; the road toward AGI gets clearer.
